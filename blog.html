MARSIS Archive Released to Public

September 6, 2023

The Mars Advanced Radar for Subsurface and Ionospheric Sounding (MARSIS) is a multi-frequency synthetic aperture radar sounder on board the Mars Express Orbiter. MARSIS been collecting data about the surface and subsurface of Mars since 2005. A new algorithm was developed to estimate and correct for the Martian ionosphere's distortion of radar signals. Using this algorithm, I generated the new optimized version of the MARSIS data archive for delivery to NASA's Planetary Data System. It has now been released online and is available to the public under "Optimized MARSIS Radargram Data": https://pds-geosciences.wustl.edu/missions/mars_express/marsis.htm

EXCALIBUR at Sagan Summer Workshop

July 29, 2023

Consistently processed exoplanet transit spectra are essential to studies of comparative planetology. The EXoplanet CALIbration and Bayesian Unified Retrieval (EXCALIBUR) pipeline performs uniform data reduction of exoplanet transit observations. The EXCALIBUR team presented its comparative planetology tool to the public at the Sagan Summer Workshop in July. A recording of my talk is available on YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wOeJiizzXdg&feature=youtu.be

The full schedule is accessible via https://nexsci.caltech.edu/workshop/2023/excalibur.shtml.
