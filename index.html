Hi, I'm Kate! I love mountains, space, and meeting people.

About

I currently study computer science and environmental sciences at the University of Virginia. I'm an intern at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory, where I am creating the next processed version of subsurface radar sounding data from the Mars Express Orbiter's MARSIS instrument. I love planetary geology and am especially interested in Europa. Last year, in an atmospheric chemistry lab at UVA, I made a prototype for an air pollution injustice map of Houston, Texas. My research project focused on making satellite observations of air pollution more accessible. Before that, I interned at IBSS, where I developed a customer feedback system integrated with Salesforce. In my free time, I love exploring new places, taking photos, and going on hikes. While on campus, I write for the humor section of the Cavalier Daily, UVA's student newspaper, and I lead weekly improv comedy lessons.
